Per Recital (88), parties supplying tools, services, components or processes integrated into high-risk AI systems must provide necessary information, capabilities, technical access and other assistance by written agreement, based on the state of the art, without compromising their own IP or trade secrets. Per Recital (89), third parties making tools/services/processes/AI components (other than GPAI models) accessible under free and open-source licences are NOT mandated to comply with value chain requirements. However, monetised components (including via technical support, software platforms, or personal data use beyond security/compatibility) lose this exception, except for microenterprise transactions. Per Recital (101), GPAI model providers have specific transparency and documentation obligations towards downstream providers and the AI Office. Per Recital (79), ultimately, a specific natural or legal person defined as the provider takes responsibility for market placement, regardless of whether they designed or developed the system.
