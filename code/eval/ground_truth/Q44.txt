Per Recital (141), real-world testing outside a sandbox requires: informed consent of participants (except for law enforcement where consent would prevent testing), a real-world testing plan submitted to market surveillance authority, EU database registration, time limitations, written agreement defining roles and responsibilities, effective oversight by competent personnel, additional safeguards for vulnerable groups, reversibility of AI decisions, personal data deletion upon consent withdrawal, data transfer to third countries only with appropriate safeguards, and distinction between testing consent and data protection consent. Per Recitals (138-139), regulatory sandboxes provide a controlled environment under strict regulatory oversight for pre-marketing testing, with mandatory establishment by each Member State (at least one at national level, physical/digital/hybrid). The sandbox aims for regulatory learning, legal certainty, and accelerated market access including for SMEs. The distinction is that sandboxes provide an institutional framework with ongoing regulatory supervision, while real-world testing provides independent testing with procedural safeguards but without embedded regulatory oversight structure.
