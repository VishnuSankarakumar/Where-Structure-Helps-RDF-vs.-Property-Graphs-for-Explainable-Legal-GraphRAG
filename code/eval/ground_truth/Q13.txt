Per Recitals (15) and (17), biometric verification (authentication) confirms that a person is who they claim to be for accessing services, unlocking devices, or security access to premises. Remote biometric identification identifies persons without their active involvement by comparing biometric data against a reference database. This distinction is critical because remote biometric identification systems are classified as high-risk (Recital 54) and their real-time use in public spaces for law enforcement is largely prohibited (Recital 32-33), whereas biometric verification is explicitly excluded from these higher-risk classifications due to its minor impact on fundamental rights.
