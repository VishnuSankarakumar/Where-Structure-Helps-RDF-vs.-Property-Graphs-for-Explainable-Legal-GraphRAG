In real-time systems, the capturing of biometric data, comparison, and identification occur instantaneously, near-instantaneously, or without a significant delay, involving live or near-live material such as video footage from cameras. In post systems, the biometric data has already been captured and the comparison and identification occur only after a significant delay, involving material such as previously recorded CCTV footage or pictures. The Regulation explicitly states there should be no scope for circumventing rules on real-time use by providing for minor delays.
