Per Recital (60), AI assisting with examination of asylum applications including reliability of evidence is classified as high-risk. Such AI must never be used to circumvent obligations under the 1951 Geneva Convention, infringe non-refoulement, or deny safe legal avenues into the Union (Recital 60, page 18). Per Recital (73), human oversight includes in-built constraints, operator responsiveness, and competent assigned persons, though the dual-verification requirement for biometric identification may be disapplied where disproportionate under Union/national law for asylum contexts. Per Recital (131), these systems must be registered in the secure non-public section of the EU database, accessible only to the Commission and relevant market surveillance authorities for their national section. Per Recital (159), market surveillance authorities for biometric AI in migration/asylum must have effective investigative and corrective powers including access to all personal data and necessary information, with complete independence.
