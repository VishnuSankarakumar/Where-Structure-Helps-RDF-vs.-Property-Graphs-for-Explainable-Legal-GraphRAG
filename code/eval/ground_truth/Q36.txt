Per Recital (76), providers of high-risk AI systems must take suitable cybersecurity measures addressing AI-specific vulnerabilities (data poisoning, adversarial attacks, membership inference). Per Recital (77), systems under horizontal cybersecurity legislation may demonstrate AI Act cybersecurity compliance by fulfilling that legislation's essential requirements, with compliance demonstrated through an EU declaration of conformity. Per Recital (78), the AI Act conformity assessment procedure applies to cybersecurity requirements of the horizontal legislation for high-risk AI systems, BUT for critical products with digital elements where the AI Act's internal-control-based assessment applies, the horizontal cybersecurity legislation's conformity assessment provisions take precedence for cybersecurity (to avoid reducing assurance levels). For all other AI Act aspects, the internal control procedure still applies. Per Recital (122), high-risk AI systems certified under a cybersecurity scheme per Regulation 2019/881 are presumed to comply with AI Act cybersecurity requirements insofar as the certificate covers them.
