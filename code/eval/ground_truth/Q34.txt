Per Recital (133), providers must embed technical solutions for machine-readable marking and detection of AI-generated content (watermarks, metadata, cryptographic methods, fingerprints), except for assistive functions or non-substantial alterations. Per Recital (134), deployers creating deep fakes must clearly disclose artificial creation/manipulation by labelling output and disclosing artificial origin, though exceptions exist for evidently creative, satirical, artistic or fictional works (limited to appropriate disclosure). AI-generated text for public interest informing must also be disclosed unless human-reviewed with editorial responsibility. Per Recitals (120)/(136), very large online platforms and search engines under the Digital Services Act must identify and mitigate systemic risks from AI-generated/manipulated content, including risks to democratic processes and disinformation. The AI Act labelling requirement does not affect the DSA obligation to process notices about illegal content under Article 16(6) of Regulation 2022/2065.
