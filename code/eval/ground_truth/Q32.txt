Per Recital (42), risk assessments based SOLELY on profiling or assessing personality traits and characteristics (nationality, residence, debt level, etc.) without reasonable suspicion based on objective verifiable facts and without human assessment are prohibited. Per Recital (59), AI for assessing reoffending risk is high-risk insofar as NOT prohibited, meaning it is permitted only when NOT based solely on profiling or personality assessment. The system must incorporate objective verifiable facts and human assessment. Recital (42) carves out risk analytics NOT based on individual profiling, such as financial fraud detection based on suspicious transactions. Thus a law enforcement reoffending assessment tool is: prohibited if based solely on profiling/personality; high-risk (permitted with full compliance) if it incorporates objective evidence and human oversight; and completely outside this specific prohibition if it analyses non-individual patterns.
