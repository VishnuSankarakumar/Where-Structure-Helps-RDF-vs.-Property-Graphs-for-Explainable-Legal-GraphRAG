The Act creates genuine ambiguity. Ne bis in idem traditionally prevents double punishment for the same offence, but the Act defines multiple distinct obligation categories (prohibitions, high-risk requirements, transparency, GPAI obligations) that could all be violated by one system. Recital (168) instructs consideration of nature, gravity, duration, consequences, and provider size, suggesting individual assessment per violation. The reference to 'upper limits for setting the administrative fines for certain specific infringements' implies distinct caps per infringement type, not a single aggregate cap. However, the ne bis in idem reference creates tension: if a company's GPAI model powers a prohibited AI practice, is the prohibition violation the 'same offence' as the GPAI transparency failure? The Act does not explicitly resolve whether violations are assessed per obligation category, per AI system, per instance of deployment, or per affected person. The EDPS has separate penalty powers for Union institutions (Recital 168), and national authorities have their own, creating potential jurisdictional overlap. A compliance officer would need to map each violation to a specific obligation category and argue whether the ne bis in idem protection applies across categories or only within them.
