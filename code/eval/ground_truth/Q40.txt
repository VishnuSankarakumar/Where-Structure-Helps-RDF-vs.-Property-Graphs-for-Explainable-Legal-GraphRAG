Per Recital (172), the whistleblower is protected under Directive (EU) 2019/1937 on the protection of persons reporting breaches. Per Recital (170), any natural or legal person considering there has been an infringement can lodge a complaint to the relevant market surveillance authority. Per Recital (168), Member States must lay down effective, proportionate and dissuasive penalties respecting ne bis in idem, with upper limits for administrative fines for specific infringements. The EDPS has power to impose fines on Union institutions. When assessing fines, relevant circumstances include the nature, gravity and duration of the infringement, its consequences, and the size of the provider (including SME considerations). Per Recital (174), the Commission must annually assess the need to amend the list of prohibited practices, providing ongoing review. The prohibited practice should already have been in force from 2 February 2025 (Recital 179), so enforcement should be fully operational.
