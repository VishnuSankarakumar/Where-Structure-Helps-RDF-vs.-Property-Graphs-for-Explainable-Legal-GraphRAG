Per Recital (58), AI for health/life insurance risk assessment and pricing for natural persons can significantly impact livelihoods and may infringe fundamental rights including financial exclusion. It is classified as high-risk. However, AI systems for detecting fraud in financial services and calculating capital requirements for credit institutions and insurance undertakings are NOT high-risk. Requirements include risk management, data governance with bias mitigation (Recital 67, particularly important given discrimination risks based on race, gender, disability, age), technical documentation, transparency, human oversight, and robustness (Recital 66). The deployer must inform affected natural persons, recognise their right to explanation (Recital 93), and conduct a fundamental rights impact assessment identifying affected groups and specific risks (Recital 96). Per Recital (158), insurance supervisory authorities under Directive 2009/138/EC are designated as competent market surveillance authorities with integration of AI Act requirements into existing supervisory frameworks, with limited derogations for quality management and monitoring.
