Per Recital (58), AI evaluating creditworthiness of natural persons is high-risk because it determines access to financial resources and essential services like housing. AI for detecting fraud and prudential capital calculations is NOT high-risk. Credit scoring for housing loans is clearly within high-risk scope. Per Recital (67), training data must be relevant, representative, and as free of errors as possible, with specific attention to bias mitigation that could affect fundamental rights or lead to discrimination based on racial/ethnic origins, gender, disability, age, or sexual orientation. Per Recital (70), providers may exceptionally process special categories of personal data for bias detection and correction under Article 9(2)(g) of GDPR. Per Recital (72), the system must include instructions of use with capabilities, limitations, and risk circumstances. Per Recital (93), the deployer must inform natural persons they are subject to the high-risk AI system and its intended purpose. Per Recital (171), affected persons have the right to a clear and meaningful explanation of decisions based on the system's output that produce legal effects or significantly affect them.
