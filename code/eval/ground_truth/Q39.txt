Per Recital (73), biometric identification systems require enhanced human oversight: no action or decision may be taken by the deployer based on the identification result unless separately verified and confirmed by at least two natural persons. This dual verification can be from one or more entities and includes the system operator. The verifications can be automatically recorded in system logs. However, for law enforcement, migration, border control and asylum, this dual requirement does not apply where Union or national law considers it disproportionate. Per Recital (35), no decision producing an adverse legal effect may be taken based solely on remote biometric identification output. Per Recital (95), post-remote biometric identification must be proportionate, legitimate, strictly necessary and targeted in terms of individuals, location, temporal scope, and based on closed legally acquired video footage. It must not lead to indiscriminate surveillance and must not circumvent real-time biometric identification prohibitions.
