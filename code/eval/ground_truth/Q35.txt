Per Recital (62), AI systems for organising, optimising and structuring political campaigns from an administrative/logistical point of view are explicitly excepted from the high-risk classification for election-influencing AI. However, if the system is found to directly influence voting behaviour or election outcomes, it falls squarely into the high-risk category. This change in use triggers the substantial modification/intended purpose change mechanism (Recital 84). The applicable requirements include: risk management system, data governance, technical documentation, transparency with instructions of use showing capabilities and limitations (Recital 72), human oversight measures including in-built constraints and operator responsiveness (Recital 73), and accuracy/robustness/cybersecurity (Recital 66). As a system likely used by public entities or affecting democratic processes, a fundamental rights impact assessment is required (Recital 96), including identification of affected groups, specific risk identification, and measures for risk materialisation.
