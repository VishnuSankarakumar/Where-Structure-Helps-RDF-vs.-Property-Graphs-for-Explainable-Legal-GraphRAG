Per Recital (155), serious incidents include: death or serious health damage, serious and irreversible disruption of critical infrastructure management/operation, infringements of fundamental rights protection obligations under Union law, or serious damage to property or the environment. All providers must have post-market monitoring systems to improve systems and take corrective action, including analysis of interaction with other AI systems and devices. They must report serious incidents to relevant authorities. Per Recital (153), each Member State designates at least one market surveillance authority as the supervision point. Per Recital (161), when an AI system is built on a GPAI model and both are provided by the same provider, supervision occurs at Union level through the AI Office. For different providers, national market surveillance authorities remain responsible for AI system supervision but can request AI Office assistance when unable to access information about the underlying GPAI model. Market surveillance authorities cooperate with the AI Office for GPAI systems usable for high-risk purposes.
