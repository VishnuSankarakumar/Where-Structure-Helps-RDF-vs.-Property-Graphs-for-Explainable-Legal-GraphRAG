Per Recital (57), AI for recruitment, decisions on work terms, promotion, termination, task allocation based on individual traits, and worker monitoring is high-risk. Per Recital (92), the Regulation does not override existing worker consultation obligations under Directive 2002/14/EC but establishes additional information requirements for planned workplace deployment of high-risk AI. Per Recital (91), the deployer must ensure use per instructions, monitoring, and record-keeping, with competent staff having adequate AI literacy and authority (Recital 91/25). Per Recital (73), human oversight must include in-built operational constraints, operator responsiveness, and competent assigned persons. Per Recital (9), the Regulation does not affect Union social policy, national labour law, including health and safety, employer-worker relationships, right to strike, or collective bargaining. Per Recital (171), affected workers have the right to a clear and meaningful explanation where the deployer's decision produces legal effects or significantly affects them.
