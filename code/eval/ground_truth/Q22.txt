Recital (61) classifies as high-risk AI systems used by judicial authorities to research and interpret facts and law and apply law to specific facts, including alternative dispute resolution with legal effects. However, AI for purely ancillary administrative activities (anonymisation, pseudonymisation, communication between personnel, administrative tasks) is NOT high-risk. Per Recital (72), high-risk systems must be designed so deployers understand how they work, with instructions of use covering capabilities, limitations, known risk circumstances, and human oversight measures. Thus judicial AI systems for legal reasoning face both the high-risk classification and the full transparency/instructions-of-use requirements, while administrative judicial AI systems face neither.
