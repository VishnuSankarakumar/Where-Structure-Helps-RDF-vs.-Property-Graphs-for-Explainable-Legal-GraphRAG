Per Recital (84), modifying the intended purpose of a non-high-risk AI system so it becomes high-risk makes the modifier the new provider. Migration/asylum AI is high-risk per Recital (60). The new provider must undertake full conformity assessment (Recital 123), implement risk management, data governance, technical documentation, transparency, human oversight, robustness and cybersecurity requirements (Recital 66). Per Recital (86), the original provider, even if no longer the provider for regulatory purposes, must closely cooperate, make available necessary information, provide technical access and other assistance for conformity assessment, unless it expressly excluded such change. The deployer-turned-provider must register in the EU database's secure non-public section for migration/asylum (Recital 131), and the system faces enhanced accuracy, non-discrimination and transparency requirements given the vulnerable position of affected persons (Recital 60).
